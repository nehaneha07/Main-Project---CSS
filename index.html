Know Face Masks More!

I can make you look better!

Face masks are a type of skincare product that is designed to improve the overall appearance and health of your skin. They come in various formulations, including clay masks, sheet masks, gel masks, and peel-off masks, and are used to address a wide range of skin concerns, such as dryness, acne, aging, and hyperpigmentation.

Face masks are typically applied to the face and left on for a period of time, ranging from 5-30 minutes, depending on the specific type of mask and its instructions. During this time, the mask's active ingredients work to deeply cleanse, hydrate, exfoliate, and nourish the skin, leaving it looking and feeling rejuvenated and refreshed.

Regular use of face masks can help to improve skin texture, reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, minimize the appearance of pores, and even out skin tone. They are a great addition to any skincare routine, and can be used once or twice a week to help maintain healthy, glowing skin. With so many different formulations available, there is a face mask out there for everyone, regardless of skin type or concern.

History In Making

1800s – 1890s

Ancient Origins of Face Masks

Face masks have been used for centuries in different cultures for a variety of purposes, such as religious ceremonies, beautification rituals, and medicinal treatments. Many ancient civilizations, including the Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans, used natural ingredients like honey, clay, and oils to make face masks.

1900s – 1930s

The Emergence of Modern Beauty Industry

The concept of modern face masks can be traced back to the early 20th century, when the beauty industry started to develop and market products specifically for women's skincare. At that time, face masks were considered a luxury item and were only used by the wealthy. Over time, face masks became more accessible and affordable, and the market for skincare products expanded.

1940s – Present

Technological Advancements in Face Mask Formulations Convenient

In recent years, advances in technology and ingredient research have led to the development of innovative face mask formulations that can deliver targeted results. For example, sheet masks, which originated in Korea, have become increasingly popular due to their ability to deeply penetrate the skin and provide intense hydration and nourishment.
